• Example Calculation
  • You bought a stock for $1,000 and sold it for $1,500 after 11 months, realizing a $500 profit
  • If your ordinary income tax rate is 22%, you'll owe $110 in taxes on the short-term capital gain.
  • However, if you had held the stock for over a year, your long-term capital gains tax might be lower, depending on your tax bracket.
