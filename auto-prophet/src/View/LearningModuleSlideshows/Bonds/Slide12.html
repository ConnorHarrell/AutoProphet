There are two main risks when investing in bonds: interest rates, and credit/default risk.

  • Bonds have an inverse relationship to interest rates.
  • When interest rates rise, the value of a bond falls.
  • The longer the maturity period, the higher the interest rate period is.
  • When interest and principal payments on a bond aren't made by a company, this means the bond has defaulted.
  • Typically, a higher operating income means a company is safer to invest in because there is less risk of defaulting.
